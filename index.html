Physics of Polymers

__

Basic and advanced concepts in polymer physics explained through intuitive explanations and interactive Javascript demonstrations.

__

Mukarram Tahir, Alfredo Alexander-Katz

Contents

 

Ideal Chains

Simple random walk model and Gaussian model of polymer molecules that lack long-range interactions, and computation of extensive properties like chain end-to-end distance and radius of gyration.

 

Non-ideal Chains

Capturing excluded volume effects in non-ideal polymer chains. Effect of solvent on polymer chain ideality and scaling laws.

 

Polymer Solutions

Thermodynamic properties like phase separations and fluctuations of systems containing multiple polymer chains. Illustration of Flory-Huggins theory as a simple model for calculating the free energy of polymer solutions.

 

Polymer Blends

Phase diagrams and correlation functions of systems containing multiple polymer types.

 

Block Copolymers

Polymers constructed from two or more different types of monomers are known as copolymers, and depending on the ratio and relative interactions between constituents, they assemble into a variety of important structures.

 

Polymer Gels

Network of polymer chains that are interconnected through intermolecular interactions such as covalent bonds, hydrogen bonds, or electrostatic interactions.

 

Dilute Solutions

Modeling of polymer systems with a dilute concentration of chains as Brownian motion, bead-spring model, and accounting for hydrodynamic interactions.

 

Entangled Systems

Polymer systems containing a high-density of chains. Overview of thermodynamic properties, fluctuations, modeling of reputation, and Rouse model.

 

Polymer Chemistry

The chemistry underlying polymer systems, including the chemical structure of common monomers, various polymerization reactions, and the chemistry of real-life polymers such as DNA and proteins.

 

Molecular Simulation

Overview of computational methods used for modeling polymers systems at various scales, including atomistic and coarse-grained molecular dynamics, Monte-Carlo methods, and dissipative particle dynamics.
